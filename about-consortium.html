Institutional Profile

"When our universities stop working in isolation, our research starts reshaping a continent."

Launched in 2012 at the University of Ilorin, Nigeria, evolving from an elite alliance of six founding institutions into a robust pan-African cooperative of eleven world-class universities.

The "Plus" Evolution

Transitioned from the core U6 framework to U6 Plus to integrate expanding memberships across sub-Saharan regional hubs, welcoming the University of Abuja as the newest member.

Impact: Herbal Research

Operationalized the premium Herbal Research Centre at the University of Ilorin, generating 15 multi-institutional peer-reviewed collaborative scientific articles to date.

Bilateral Strength

Replacing fractured, dual-institution partnerships with scaled multi-lateral alliances, amplifying structural leverage on global funding grids.

Strategic Intent

Vision

Leadership in innovation and sustainability across the higher education landscapes of Africa.

Core Mandate

Mission

To aggressively foster comprehensive collaboration in cross-border education and transformative scientific research.

Philosophical Base

Values

Excellence Inclusivity Innovation

Statutory Objectives

  • Promote and develop a new spirit of co-operation among member universities so that they may work collectively.
  • Strengthen the respective academic entities of the Universities in line with their specific Missions.
  • Facilitate international educational exchange and the participation in joint academic and educational ventures through partnerships.
  • Provide opportunities to enhance each institution and promote cross-cultural understanding.
  • Implement programs and activities that are consistent with the Missions of the respective institutions.
Statutory Instrument

U6+ Constitution

Ratified initially in Calabar (2019) following an intensive two-year preparatory process backed by administrative legal units. Systematically reviewed and amended at the Annual General Meetings in 2024 and 2025 to optimize operational responsiveness.

Executive Treaty

Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)

A binding, unified memorandum formalizing cross-border research exchanges and credit architectures. The finalized, modern iterations stand universally adopted and authorized under direct signatures from the Vice-Chancellors of all member institutions.
